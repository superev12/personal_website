Born in Adelaide, South Australia, I grew up between Bristol and Cambridge, then studied a MEng in Computer Science at the University of York. I'm now based in Cambridge (the UK one), working as a freelance software engineer.

I've been part of lots of software teams in different sectors, such as [ARM](https://www.arm.com), Eight19, [Emberion](https://www.emberion.com), [Rapita](https://www.rapitasystems.com), [Genomics](https://www.genomics.com), Premium Lithium, [Songbook Systems](https://www.songbook-pro.com), and [Open Science Tools](https://opensciencetools.org/). I'm currently working on a project of my own, [NoteTamer](/posts/notetamer), a sight-reading practice app.

I'm particularly interested in Music Software and how it can better suit the needs of musicians. I've worked with [Lore Lixenberg](https://www.lorelixenberg.art), [Tom Collins](https://www.tomcollinsresearch.net/), and Matthew Hall to create custom music software for performances. I'm also interested in the broader way that software affects the sort of music that we make, particularly regarding tuning, microtonality, and non-western temperaments.

I'm also a committed follower of Jesus, and help lead worship at my church in Cambridge.
